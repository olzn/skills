# Biases & Critique

The bias sweep for Phase 3, and the entry point for **Critique mode** (when someone
brings reasoning they've already formed and wants it stress-tested). Sources: Duke,
Tetlock, and the *Great Mental Models* biases material.

## Named biases

Each entry: the **tell** (how it shows up) and the **corrective question**.

### Resulting
- **Tell:** Rating a decision good or bad based on how it turned out (Duke).
- **Corrective:** "Judge the process at the time, not the result — what did you
  actually know and choose before the outcome was known?"

### Hindsight / memory creep
- **Tell:** "I knew it all along" / "I should have known"; the past feels inevitable
  (Duke).
- **Corrective:** "Was that really knowable beforehand, or did it only reveal itself
  after the fact?"

### Motivated reasoning
- **Tell:** Evidence gets processed to protect a belief you already hold; smart
  people do this *better* (Duke/Tetlock).
- **Corrective:** "What would convince you you're wrong — and have you gone looking
  for it?"

### Self-serving bias
- **Tell:** Crediting skill for wins, blaming luck for losses (and reversing it for
  other people) (Duke).
- **Corrective:** "If this exact result had happened to someone else, how would you
  field it — skill or luck?"

### Confirmation bias
- **Tell:** Seeking and weighting evidence that fits; discounting what doesn't
  (Tetlock).
- **Corrective:** "What's the strongest evidence *against* your view, and have you
  steelmanned it?"

### Scope insensitivity
- **Tell:** Your answer doesn't move when the magnitude or time frame changes
  (Tetlock).
- **Corrective:** "If the stakes or the deadline doubled, would your number actually
  change?"

### Sunk cost
- **Tell:** Continuing because of what's already spent rather than the forward payoff
  (Duke).
- **Corrective:** "Ignoring everything already invested, would you choose this path
  starting from today?"

### Anchoring
- **Tell:** Over-relying on the first number considered and under-adjusting from it
  (Tetlock).
- **Corrective:** "Is your starting point a meaningful base rate, or an arbitrary
  anchor you happened to see first?"

### Overconfidence
- **Tell:** Ranges too narrow; certainty where the evidence doesn't warrant it
  (Tetlock).
- **Corrective:** "Would you actually bet at those odds — and would the shock test
  widen your range?"

### Availability / recency
- **Tell:** Over-weighting whatever is vivid or recent (GMM).
- **Corrective:** "Are you reasoning from a representative sample, or from the one
  story that's top of mind?"

### Status-quo bias
- **Tell:** Defaulting to the current state because change feels riskier than it is
  (Duke/GMM).
- **Corrective:** "If you weren't already here, would you actively choose this — or
  is inertia deciding for you?"

### The wrong-side-of-maybe fallacy
- **Tell:** Calling a probabilistic forecast "wrong" because the event didn't happen
  (Tetlock).
- **Corrective:** "A 70% call isn't refuted by one outcome — are you grading the
  forecast or the result?"

## Critique-mode checklist

Run these in sequence against reasoning the person has already formed. Each names its
source lightly; stop early if the decision is plainly low-stakes.

1. **Resulting check** — "Are you judging this by the decision or by the outcome?"
   (Duke)
2. **Put a number on it** — "Pick a probability for each key belief. Would you bet
   on it at those odds?" (Tetlock)
3. **Outside view** — "What's the base rate? How often do things like this turn out
   this way in situations like this?" (Tetlock)
4. **Premortem** — "Assume this failed. What are the most likely reasons — split
   skill from luck." (Duke)
5. **Motivated reasoning / confirmation** — "What evidence would change your mind,
   and have you actually looked for it?" (Tetlock)
6. **Missing perspective** — "Whose view is absent here? What would the person who
   most disagrees say?" (Tetlock's dragonfly eye)
7. **Scope test** — "Does your confidence move when the stakes or time frame change?
   If not, why not?" (Tetlock)
8. **Circle of competence** — "Are you inside the domain where your judgment is
   actually reliable, or should an expert weigh in?" (GMM)
